Serenity Suites Coastal Resort

Projects / Hospitality / Serenity Suites Coastal Resort
Hospitality Architecture

Eight villas tuned to a Pacific bluff.

An eight-villa coastal resort tucked into a Pacific bluff outside Half Moon Bay. The owners wanted a property that felt private and contemporary without the brittleness modern hospitality can carry. Each villa frames a different relationship with the ocean, close, distant, hidden, so no two stays feel the same.

Year

2023

Location

Pacific Shores, California

Size

18,400 sq ft (8 villas)

Status

Completed

Services

Architecture, Interior, Landscape

Client

Coastal Hospitality Group

The brief

A boutique resort on a constrained coastal site

A boutique hospitality client returning to the Bay Area after a decade of large-scale work in the Pacific Northwest. The site was a former agricultural parcel above a tidal cove with strict coastal commission constraints, a height limit that effectively required a single-story design, and a community that had pushed back on every previous attempt to build on the parcel.

Our approach

Eight villas, each framing a different view

Eight villas threaded through the existing tree line, each oriented around a different framed view rather than chasing the same panoramic clichés. Cross-ventilation, deep eaves, and outdoor showers do the work air conditioning would have done elsewhere, the property has no mechanical cooling at all. A shared bathhouse and dining pavilion anchor the central walkway.
