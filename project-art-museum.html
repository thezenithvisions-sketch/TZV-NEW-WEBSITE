Art Museum Expansion

Projects / Cultural / Art Museum Expansion
Cultural Architecture

A glass volume floating over the beaux-arts.

A 40,000 sq ft contemporary wing added to a beaux-arts museum without competing with it. A cantilevered glass volume floats above the historic building, the connection knitted by a quiet steel-and-glass bridge.

Year

2023

Location

Chicago, Illinois

Size

40,000 sq ft

Status

Completed

Services

Architecture, Lighting

Client

Chicago Institute of Fine Arts

The brief

A modern wing for a beaux-arts museum

A growing modern collection that had outgrown the museum’s 1923 main building. The trustees were divided — half wanted a bold contemporary statement, the other half wanted a sympathetic reproduction. The site was a tight footprint adjacent to the historic structure with a major sewer line directly beneath.

Our approach

Thread the new wing above, leave the facade untouched

We threaded the new wing above the existing building as a cantilevered volume, leaving the historic facade untouched at street level. A 60-foot steel truss carries the load to two new pile caps that thread between the existing utilities. Inside, a deliberate sequence of compressed-and-expanded volumes lets the curators stage the collection as a journey.
