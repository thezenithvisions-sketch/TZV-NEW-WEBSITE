Hillside Oasis Residence

Projects / Private Residence / Hillside Oasis Residence
Private Residence Architecture & Interior

Built into the cliff, not on top of it.

A 9,800 sq ft family home commissioned for a Pacific cliffside in Malibu. The brief asked for a home that would seem to grow out of the rock itself, at the same time providing every modern comfort and an outdoor yoga pavilion that opens to the ocean. We answered with local stone, board-formed concrete, and a roof line that traces the topography.

The brief

A coastal home that disappears into the landscape

A coastal family with three children wanted a primary residence that didn’t compete with the landscape. The site was steep, exposed, and west-facing, the house would catch the full afternoon sun and the full force of winter storms. Privacy from the road above and neighbors below was non-negotiable.

Our approach

Stepped section, local stone, a cantilevered pavilion

We responded with a stepped section that tucks half the floor area below the existing grade. Local Topanga slate forms the lower retaining walls, board-formed concrete shapes the upper massing, and the roof line traces the cliff edge so the house reads as a continuation of the geology. The cantilevered yoga pavilion that the client jokingly asked for became the project’s signature, a 22-foot timber-and-glass platform floating over the ocean.
