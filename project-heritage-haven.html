Heritage Haven Restoration

Projects / Historic / Heritage Haven Restoration
Historic Restoration

Invisible by design.

A meticulous restoration of an 1890s townhouse, returning it to its original Italianate plan while quietly integrating modern systems. Period plasterwork, reclaimed millwork, and an entirely new mechanical core — invisible by design.

Year

2022

Location

Boston, Massachusetts

Size

8,400 sq ft

Status

Completed

Services

Restoration, Interior, MEP

Client

Boston Historic Trust

The brief

Restore an Italianate landmark

A landmark 1890s Italianate townhouse chopped into six apartments in the 1970s. The trust wanted to restore it as a public-facing museum and event space while keeping all life-safety systems hidden behind period finishes. Original drawings were partial; archaeology was the only way through.

Our approach

Phased restoration, hidden modern systems

A phased restoration starting with structural work and a clean reading of the original plan. We threaded the new MEP through restored chases, used reclaimed pine for floor patching, and matched the original lime-based plaster with a craftsperson trained in the period technique. Every visible surface is either original or indistinguishable from it.
