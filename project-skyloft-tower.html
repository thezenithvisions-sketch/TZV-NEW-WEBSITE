Skyloft Office Tower

Projects / Commercial / Skyloft Office Tower
Commercial Mass Timber

Steel below, timber above.

A 28-story office tower with a 7-story timber crown and a public market at grade. The mass-timber upper floors carry less embodied carbon than a comparable steel design, and the ground floor returns space to the city.

Year

2024

Location

Seattle, Washington

Size

380,000 sq ft (28 stories)

Status

Completed

Services

Architecture, Structural collaboration, Sustainability

Client

Skyloft Properties

The brief

A lower-carbon flagship for downtown

A speculative office developer that had committed publicly to a lower-carbon construction strategy for their new flagship. The site was a half-block in downtown Seattle with strong floor-area ratio limits and a community demand for active ground-floor uses.

Our approach

Hybrid construction, public ground floor

A hybrid structure with conventional steel-and-concrete for the first 21 floors and a 7-story mass-timber crown above — bringing the building’s overall embodied carbon below a comparable all-steel design by about 38%. The ground floor is given over to a public market hall with rentable food stalls and a shared seating commons.
