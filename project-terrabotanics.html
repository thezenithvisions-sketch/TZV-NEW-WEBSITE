TerraBotanics Grand Botanical

Projects / Cultural / TerraBotanics Grand Botanical
Cultural Landscape

Architecture as a quiet servant of the landscape.

A visitor pavilion and conservatory for a public botanical garden. Mass-timber arches form a canopy, and a rainwater system feeds the gardens it sits within, architecture as a quiet servant of the landscape.

Year

2022

Location

Portland, Oregon

Size

28,000 sq ft

Status

Completed

Services

Architecture, Landscape, Sustainability

Client

TerraBotanics Foundation

The brief

A pavilion for a public botanical garden

An established botanical garden expanding its public programming. The board wanted a visitor pavilion that could host school groups and a conservatory that could accommodate the foundation’s new tropical collection. The site sat at the seam between the existing garden and a stand of mature Douglas firs.

Our approach

Mass-timber canopy, net-zero rainwater

A glulam timber pavilion with a stepped canopy that frames views into the existing garden, paired with a curved glasshouse that nests against the firs. The rainwater system is the project’s quiet centerpiece, every roof surface drains to a planted bioswale that feeds the garden’s irrigation, eliminating municipal water demand entirely.
